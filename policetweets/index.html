The DC police maintain a twitter account at @DCPoliceDept that reports near-real-time events with brief synopses. This site is a catalogue of their public postings; the text, as a government production, is public domain.

In September 2011, the DC police department encrypted their radio communications and the Fire/EMS service began filtering what they post on twitter. Radio scanners were previously useful for journalists keeping track of law enforcement.

On the right is a search box: it accepts both standard queries and regular expressions. If your query has a matching group, like the query (\d+lbs), the matches will be made into a chart on the right side of the page, showing number of occurrences for each match.
